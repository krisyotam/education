## History of Psychology

### Declarative
- Structuralism (Wundt, Titchener), Functionalism (James, Dewey), Behaviorism (Watson, Skinner), Gestalt (Wertheimer, Kohler, Koffka)
- Psychoanalysis (Freud, Jung, Adler, Klein, Winnicott, Lacan)
- Humanistic psychology (Maslow, Rogers)
- Cognitive revolution (1950s-60s: Chomsky's review of Skinner, Miller, Broadbent, Neisser)
- Social cognition, embodied cognition, computational/connectionist approaches
- Key experiments: Milgram obedience, Zimbardo prison, Asch conformity, Harlow attachment, Bandura Bobo doll, Pavlov, Little Albert

### Theory
- **Textbook**: *A History of Modern Psychology* by Goodwin — comprehensive
- **Textbook**: *An Introduction to the History of Psychology* by Hergenhahn & Henley
- **Primary**: William James *Principles of Psychology* (selections, esp. chapters on habit, stream of consciousness, emotion, the self)
- **Primary**: Freud — *Introductory Lectures on Psychoanalysis*, *The Interpretation of Dreams* (selections), "The Ego and the Id"
- **Primary**: Watson "Psychology as the Behaviorist Views It" (1913)
- **Primary**: Skinner *Science and Human Behavior*

## Research Methods

### Declarative
- Experimental design: independent/dependent/confound variables, random assignment, control groups, within/between subjects
- Quasi-experimental designs, correlational studies, longitudinal/cross-sectional
- Validity: internal, external, construct, statistical conclusion
- Reliability: test-retest, inter-rater, internal consistency (Cronbach's alpha)
- Survey design: question wording, sampling methods, response biases
- Qualitative methods: interviews, thematic analysis, grounded theory, phenomenological analysis
- Ethics: IRB, informed consent, deception, debriefing, APA ethics code

### Procedural
- Design an experiment with proper controls
- Conduct a power analysis (G*Power)
- Write an APA-style research report
- Preregister a study on OSF (https://osf.io/)
- Conduct a literature review using PsycINFO

### Theory
- **Textbook**: *Research Methods in Psychology* by Morling — accessible
- **Textbook**: *Research Design in Clinical Psychology* by Kazdin
- **Textbook**: *Experimental and Quasi-Experimental Designs for Generalized Causal Inference* by Shadish, Cook, Campbell — the gold standard for causal inference
- **Textbook**: *Qualitative Research in Psychology* by Willig
- **APA Publication Manual** (7th edition) — essential for writing

## Statistics

### Declarative
- Descriptive statistics: mean, median, mode, SD, variance, skewness, kurtosis
- Parametric tests: t-test (one-sample, independent, paired), one-way ANOVA, factorial ANOVA, repeated measures ANOVA, ANCOVA
- Post-hoc tests: Tukey, Bonferroni, Scheffe
- Correlation (Pearson, Spearman), simple and multiple regression, logistic regression
- Non-parametric: chi-square, Mann-Whitney U, Wilcoxon, Kruskal-Wallis, Friedman
- Effect sizes: Cohen's d, eta-squared, odds ratio, r
- Power analysis, Type I and Type II errors, p-values, confidence intervals
- Advanced: multilevel/hierarchical linear modeling (HLM), structural equation modeling (SEM), factor analysis (EFA/CFA), mediation/moderation, Bayesian statistics, meta-analysis

### Procedural
- **R tutorials**: https://www.r-project.org/ — increasingly the standard
- **JASP**: https://jasp-stats.org/ — free, Bayesian-friendly alternative to SPSS
- **jamovi**: https://www.jamovi.org/ — free, R-based GUI
- Practice with real datasets: OSF, Kaggle, ICPSR
- Andy Field's *Discovering Statistics* — worked examples in R or SPSS
- Run a complete analysis pipeline: data cleaning → assumptions → analysis → visualization → interpretation

### Theory
- **Textbook**: *Discovering Statistics Using R* by Andy Field — the most popular stats text for psychologists, engaging
- **Textbook**: *Statistical Methods for Psychology* by Howell — more traditional, thorough
- **Textbook**: *Applied Multiple Regression/Correlation Analysis for the Behavioral Sciences* by Cohen, Cohen, West, Aiken — the regression bible
- **Textbook**: *Statistical Power Analysis for the Behavioral Sciences* by Jacob Cohen — foundational
- **Textbook**: *An Introduction to Statistical Learning* (ISLR) — for modern ML approaches in psych: https://www.statlearning.com/
- **Textbook**: *Data Analysis Using Regression and Multilevel/Hierarchical Models* by Gelman & Hill — essential for advanced work
- **Textbook**: *Principles and Practice of Structural Equation Modeling* by Kline — SEM standard
- **Textbook**: *Introduction to Mediation, Moderation, and Conditional Process Analysis* by Andrew Hayes — widely used
- **Coursera**: *Statistics with R* (Duke University)

## Cognitive Psychology

### Declarative
- Attention: selective (cocktail party, Broadbent/Treisman models), divided, sustained, attentional blink, change blindness, inattentional blindness
- Memory: sensory, short-term/working memory (Baddeley's model), long-term (episodic, semantic, procedural), encoding (levels of processing, generation effect), retrieval (recall, recognition, cued), forgetting (interference, decay, motivated), false memories, memory consolidation
- Language: phonology, syntax, semantics, pragmatics, Chomsky's UG, connectionist models, bilingualism, Sapir-Whorf
- Problem solving and reasoning: heuristics and biases (Kahneman & Tversky), mental models, analogical reasoning, deductive vs inductive, dual-process theory (System 1/System 2)
- Decision making: prospect theory, framing effects, anchoring, availability, representativeness
- Concepts and categories: prototype theory (Rosch), exemplar theory, theory-theory
- Expertise: deliberate practice (Ericsson), chunking

### Procedural
- Design a cognitive psychology experiment (e.g., Stroop task, memory test)
- Replicate classic findings (e.g., levels-of-processing effect, anchoring bias)
- Run experiments using PsychoPy or jsPsych
- Analyze reaction time and accuracy data

### Theory
- **MIT 9.00 — Introduction to Psychology** (includes cognitive): https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/9-00sc-introduction-to-psychology-fall-2011/
- **Textbook**: *Cognitive Psychology: Connecting Mind, Research, and Everyday Experience* by Goldstein — accessible
- **Textbook**: *Cognitive Psychology* by Sternberg & Sternberg
- **Textbook**: *Cognitive Psychology and Its Implications* by John Anderson
- **Textbook**: *Thinking, Fast and Slow* by Daniel Kahneman — accessible classic on heuristics and biases
- **Textbook**: *The Oxford Handbook of Cognitive Psychology* — advanced reference
- **Key papers**: Baddeley & Hitch (1974) working memory; Kahneman & Tversky (1979) prospect theory; Posner & Petersen (1990) attention; Tulving (1972) episodic vs semantic memory; Roediger & McDermott (1995) DRM false memory paradigm

## Developmental Psychology

### Declarative
- Piaget: stages (sensorimotor, preoperational, concrete operational, formal operational), schemas, assimilation/accommodation, object permanence, conservation, egocentrism
- Vygotsky: zone of proximal development, scaffolding, private speech, sociocultural theory
- Attachment: Bowlby (internal working models, monotropy), Ainsworth (Strange Situation: secure, anxious-avoidant, anxious-ambivalent, disorganized)
- Moral development: Kohlberg (preconventional, conventional, postconventional), Gilligan (ethics of care), Turiel (domain theory)
- Theory of mind: false belief tasks, Sally-Anne test, autism and ToM
- Language development: babbling, one-word, telegraphic, overextension/underextension, critical period hypothesis
- Erikson: psychosocial stages (8 stages, identity vs role confusion)
- Adolescent development: brain maturation (prefrontal cortex), risk-taking, identity formation
- Aging: cognitive decline, wisdom, crystallized vs fluid intelligence, successful aging

### Procedural
- Administer developmental assessments (Bayley, WISC)
- Observe and code child behavior
- Design a longitudinal or cross-sectional developmental study

### Theory

- **Textbook**: *Developmental Psychology* by Upton — good overview
- **Textbook**: *How Children Develop* by Siegler, DeLoache, Eisenberg, Saffran
- **Textbook**: *Child Development* by Berk — comprehensive
- **Textbook**: *Lifespan Development* by Boyd & Bee
- **Textbook**: *The Scientist in the Crib* by Gopnik, Meltzoff, Kuhl — accessible on infant cognition
- **Key papers**: Ainsworth et al. (1978) Strange Situation; Baillargeon (1987) violation of expectation; Wimmer & Perner (1983) false belief

## Social Psychology

### Declarative
- Attitudes: formation, change, cognitive dissonance (Festinger), self-perception theory (Bem), elaboration likelihood model (Petty & Cacioppo)
- Conformity: Asch, informational vs normative influence, Sherif autokinetic
- Obedience: Milgram experiments, agentic state, situational factors
- Group dynamics: social facilitation, social loafing, groupthink, deindividuation, bystander effect (Darley & Latane), polarization
- Attribution: fundamental attribution error, actor-observer bias, self-serving bias, correspondence bias, Kelley's covariation model
- Prejudice and stereotypes: implicit bias (IAT), stereotype threat (Steele), minimal group paradigm (Tajfel), social identity theory, realistic conflict theory (Robbers Cave)
- Aggression: frustration-aggression hypothesis, social learning (Bandura), general aggression model
- Prosocial behavior: empathy-altruism hypothesis (Batson), reciprocal altruism, kin selection
- Self: self-concept, self-esteem, self-efficacy (Bandura), self-regulation, cultural differences (individualism/collectivism)
- Relationships: attraction (proximity, similarity, physical attractiveness), Sternberg's triangular theory, attachment styles in adults

### Procedural
- Design a social psychology experiment (e.g., test priming effects)
- Administer IAT and analyze results
- Replicate a classic social psych finding and evaluate replication success/failure
- Conduct a survey study on attitudes

### Theory
- **Coursera**: *Social Psychology* (Wesleyan University) — excellent free course
- **Textbook**: *Social Psychology* by Aronson, Wilson, Akert, Sommers — very readable
- **Textbook**: *Social Psychology* by David Myers & Jean Twenge — the most popular text
- **Textbook**: *The Social Animal* by Elliot Aronson — classic, essay-style
- **Textbook**: *Social Cognition* by Fiske & Taylor — graduate level
- **Textbook**: *Handbook of Social Psychology* (5th ed.) by Fiske, Gilbert, Lindzey — the comprehensive reference
- **Key papers**: Festinger (1957) cognitive dissonance; Milgram (1963) obedience; Darley & Latane (1968) bystander; Kahneman & Tversky throughout; Steele & Aronson (1995) stereotype threat; Greenwald et al. (1998) IAT

## Personality Psychology

### Declarative
- Trait theories: Allport, Cattell (16PF), Big Five / Five Factor Model (OCEAN: openness, conscientiousness, extraversion, agreeableness, neuroticism), HEXACO model
- Psychodynamic: Freud (id, ego, superego, defense mechanisms, psychosexual stages), Jung (archetypes, collective unconscious), Adler (inferiority complex)
- Humanistic: Maslow (hierarchy of needs, self-actualization), Rogers (unconditional positive regard, self-concept, fully functioning person)
- Social-cognitive: Bandura (self-efficacy, reciprocal determinism), Mischel (person-situation debate, CAPS model), Dweck (mindsets)
- Biological: temperament, behavioral genetics (heritability of traits), Gray's BIS/BAS
- Personality assessment: NEO-PI-R, MMPI, projective tests (Rorschach, TAT)
- Person-situation debate: Mischel vs trait theorists, interactionism

### Theory
- **Textbook**: *Personality: Theory and Research* by Cervone & Pervin
- **Textbook**: *Personality Psychology: Domains of Knowledge about Human Nature* by Larsen & Buss
- **Textbook**: *The Personality Puzzle* by David Funder — engaging
- **Key papers**: McCrae & Costa on Big Five; Mischel (1968) *Personality and Assessment*; Roberts et al. (2006) personality change across lifespan

## Sensation and Perception

### Declarative
- Vision: electromagnetic spectrum, eye anatomy (cornea, lens, retina, rods, cones, fovea), visual pathways (LGN, V1), receptive fields, feature detectors, gestalt principles, depth cues (binocular, monocular), color vision (trichromatic, opponent-process), object recognition (template, feature, structural description), face perception (FFA)
- Audition: sound properties, ear anatomy, place vs temporal coding, auditory scene analysis, speech perception
- Chemical senses: olfaction (olfactory bulb, combinatorial coding), gustation (five tastes + umami)
- Somatosensation: touch, pain (gate control theory), temperature, proprioception
- Multisensory integration: McGurk effect, rubber hand illusion

### Theory
- **Textbook**: *Sensation and Perception* by Goldstein & Brockmole — the standard
- **Textbook**: *Visual Perception* by Bruce, Green, Georgeson
- **Textbook**: *An Introduction to the Psychology of Hearing* by Moore
- **Key papers**: Hubel & Wiesel (1962) receptive fields; Kanwisher et al. (1997) FFA; McGurk & MacDonald (1976) McGurk effect

## Abnormal Psychology

### Declarative
- DSM-5-TR diagnostic categories and key criteria:
  - Anxiety disorders: GAD, social anxiety, specific phobias, panic disorder, agoraphobia
  - Mood disorders: MDD, persistent depressive disorder, bipolar I & II, cyclothymia
  - Psychotic disorders: schizophrenia (positive/negative/cognitive symptoms), schizoaffective, brief psychotic
  - OCD and related: OCD, body dysmorphic, hoarding, trichotillomania
  - Trauma and stressor-related: PTSD, acute stress disorder, adjustment disorders
  - Personality disorders: clusters A (paranoid, schizoid, schizotypal), B (antisocial, borderline, histrionic, narcissistic), C (avoidant, dependent, OCPD)
  - Eating disorders: anorexia, bulimia, binge eating
  - Substance use disorders, neurodevelopmental disorders (ADHD, ASD), dissociative disorders
- Etiological models: biological (genetics, neurotransmitter), psychological (cognitive, behavioral, psychodynamic), social (stress-diathesis), biopsychosocial
- Comorbidity patterns, prevalence rates (epidemiology)

### Procedural
- Practice differential diagnosis with case vignettes
- Learn structured diagnostic interviews (SCID, MINI)
- Risk assessment skills (suicidality, violence)

### Theory
- **Textbook**: *Abnormal Psychology* by Nolen-Hoeksema — excellent, integrative
- **Textbook**: *Abnormal Psychology* by Barlow & Durand

## Clinical Psychology

### Declarative
- Major therapeutic approaches:
  - **CBT**: cognitive model (Beck), automatic thoughts, cognitive distortions, behavioral activation, exposure therapy, cognitive restructuring
  - **DBT**: Linehan, biosocial theory, mindfulness, distress tolerance, emotion regulation, interpersonal effectiveness — for BPD and emotion dysregulation
  - **Psychodynamic**: transference, countertransference, free association, defense mechanisms, object relations, mentalization-based therapy
  - **Humanistic/Person-centered**: Rogers (empathy, unconditional positive regard, congruence), Gestalt therapy
  - **ACT**: psychological flexibility, defusion, acceptance, values, committed action
  - **EMDR**: for PTSD, bilateral stimulation, adaptive information processing model
  - **Motivational Interviewing**: stages of change, OARS skills, change talk
  - **Third-wave CBT**: mindfulness-based stress reduction (MBSR), mindfulness-based cognitive therapy (MBCT)
- Evidence-based practice: empirically supported treatments (ESTs), common factors, therapeutic alliance
- Outcome research: randomized controlled trials, effect sizes, benchmarking

### Procedural
- Learn cognitive restructuring techniques (thought records, behavioral experiments)
- Practice motivational interviewing (OARS: open questions, affirmations, reflections, summaries)
- Case formulation: develop a biopsychosocial formulation
- Conduct a clinical interview (intake, mental status exam)
- Outcome measurement: PHQ-9 (depression), GAD-7 (anxiety), PCL-5 (PTSD)

### Theory
- **Textbook**: *Clinical Psychology: Science, Practice, and Diversity* by Pomerantz
- **Textbook**: *Clinical Handbook of Psychological Disorders* by Barlow (ed.) — the treatment bible
- **Textbook**: *Cognitive Behavior Therapy: Basics and Beyond* by Judith Beck — CBT manual
- **Textbook**: *Cognitive Therapy of Depression* by Beck, Rush, Shaw, Emery — the original CBT text
- **Textbook**: *DBT Skills Training Manual* by Marsha Linehan
- **Textbook**: *ACT Made Simple* by Russ Harris — accessible introduction
- **Textbook**: *Motivational Interviewing* by Miller & Rollnick (4th ed.)
- **Textbook**: *Psychodynamic Psychotherapy: A Clinical Manual* by Cabaniss et al.
- **Textbook**: *The Heart and Soul of Change* by Duncan et al. — common factors research

## Evolutionary Psychology

### Declarative
- Natural selection applied to behavior and cognition
- Inclusive fitness, kin selection (Hamilton's rule), reciprocal altruism (Trivers)
- Parental investment theory (Trivers): differential reproductive strategies
- Mate selection: sexual selection, good genes, indicator traits
- Evolved psychological mechanisms: domain-specific modules, cheater detection (Cosmides)
- Mismatch hypothesis: evolved mechanisms in modern environments
- Criticisms: just-so stories, genetic determinism concerns, SSSM critique

### Theory

## Quantitative Psychology

### Declarative
- Classical Test Theory: true score, reliability, standard error of measurement
- Item Response Theory (IRT): 1PL, 2PL, 3PL models, item characteristic curves, differential item functioning
- Factor analysis: EFA (principal axis, maximum likelihood), CFA, communalities, eigenvalues, scree plot
- Structural equation modeling: path analysis, latent variables, fit indices (CFI, RMSEA, SRMR, chi-square)
- Meta-analysis: effect size aggregation, fixed vs random effects, publication bias (funnel plot, trim-and-fill)
- Bayesian statistics: priors, posteriors, Bayes factors, credible intervals, MCMC
- Multilevel modeling: random intercepts, random slopes, ICC, centering

### Procedural
- Run CFA and SEM in R (lavaan package) or Mplus
- Conduct a meta-analysis using R (metafor package) or Comprehensive Meta-Analysis (CMA)
- IRT analysis using R (mirt package)
- Bayesian analysis using JASP, Stan, or brms

### Theory
- **Textbook**: *Principles and Practice of Structural Equation Modeling* by Rex Kline
- **Textbook**: *Introduction to Classical and Modern Test Theory* by Crocker & Algina
- **Textbook**: *Item Response Theory for Psychologists* by Embretson & Reise
- **Textbook**: *Introduction to Meta-Analysis* by Borenstein, Hedges, Higgins, Rothstein
- **Textbook**: *Bayesian Data Analysis* by Gelman et al. — the Bayesian bible
- **Textbook**: *Statistical Rethinking* by Richard McElreath — Bayesian, excellent, free lectures on YouTube

## Replication Crisis

### Declarative
- Open Science Collaboration (2015): ~36% of psychology studies replicated
- Questionable research practices: p-hacking, HARKing, publication bias, file drawer problem
- Reforms: preregistration, registered reports, open data/materials/code, replication studies
- Many Labs projects, Registered Replication Reports
- Bayesian alternatives to NHST, estimation statistics, effect sizes over p-values

- **Paper**: Open Science Collaboration (2015) "Estimating the reproducibility of psychological science" — *Science*
