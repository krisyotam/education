## Ancient Philosophy

### Declarative
- Pre-Socratics: Thales (water), Anaximander (apeiron), Heraclitus (flux, logos), Parmenides (Being), Democritus (atoms), Empedocles (four elements), Anaxagoras (nous)
- Socrates: the examined life, elenchus (Socratic method), Socratic irony, aporia
- Plato: Theory of Forms, the Good, tripartite soul, philosopher-kings, the cave allegory, divided line, recollection (anamnesis), participation/imitation
- Aristotle: hylomorphism, four causes, potentiality/actuality, eudaimonia, the mean, syllogistic logic, categories, nous, natural teleology
- Hellenistic: Stoic physics/ethics/logic (Zeno, Chrysippus, Epictetus, Marcus Aurelius), Epicureanism (atoms, pleasure, ataraxia), Skepticism (Pyrrhonism, Academic), Neoplatonism (Plotinus: the One, emanation)

### Procedural
- Reconstruct Socratic arguments from the dialogues
- Formalize Aristotelian syllogisms
- Write a comparative essay on Plato vs Aristotle on universals
- Practice the Socratic method in dialogue form

### Theory
- **Primary texts (essential)**:
  - **Plato**: *Republic*, *Symposium*, *Phaedo*, *Phaedrus*, *Meno*, *Theaetetus*, *Parmenides*, *Timaeus*, *Apology*, *Gorgias*, *Protagoras*, *Philebus*
  - **Aristotle**: *Nicomachean Ethics*, *Politics*, *Metaphysics* (esp. Books I, IV, VII, XII), *De Anima*, *Physics* (Books I-II), *Categories*, *Posterior Analytics*, *Poetics*
  - **Pre-Socratics**: *Fragments* — Kirk, Raven & Schofield collection
  - **Stoics**: Epictetus *Discourses* & *Enchiridion*, Marcus Aurelius *Meditations*, Seneca *Letters*
  - **Epicurus**: *Letter to Menoeceus*, *Principal Doctrines*; Lucretius *De Rerum Natura*
  - **Sextus Empiricus**: *Outlines of Pyrrhonism*
  - **Plotinus**: *Enneads* (selections)
- **Secondary**:
  - *The Cambridge Companion to Plato*, *The Cambridge Companion to Aristotle*
  - Julia Annas: *An Introduction to Plato's Republic*
  - Terence Irwin: *Plato's Ethics*, *Aristotle's First Principles*
  - A.A. Long: *Hellenistic Philosophy*
  - Pierre Hadot: *Philosophy as a Way of Life*, *What Is Ancient Philosophy?*
  - G.E.R. Lloyd: *Early Greek Science*

## Medieval Philosophy

### Declarative
- Augustine: problem of evil, divine illumination, original sin, City of God vs City of Man, time and eternity
- Boethius: consolation of philosophy, universals problem
- Anselm: ontological argument, fides quaerens intellectum
- Aquinas: five ways (arguments for God), natural law, analogy of being, faith and reason, hylomorphism
- Duns Scotus: univocity of being, haecceity, formal distinction
- Ockham: nominalism, Ockham's razor, voluntarism
- Averroes: double truth, Aristotelian commentaries
- Maimonides: negative theology, Guide of the Perplexed

### Procedural
- Reconstruct Anselm's ontological argument and its critiques
- Analyze Aquinas's five ways — identify premises and logical structure
- Write on the problem of universals (realism vs nominalism)

### Theory
- **Primary texts**:
  - Augustine: *Confessions*, *City of God* (selections), *On Free Choice of the Will*
  - Boethius: *The Consolation of Philosophy*
  - Anselm: *Proslogion*
  - Aquinas: *Summa Theologiae* (selections: I, QQ. 2-3, 13; I-II, QQ. 1-5, 90-97), *Summa Contra Gentiles* (selections)
  - Duns Scotus: selections on univocity and individuation
  - Ockham: selections on universals and razor
  - Maimonides: *Guide of the Perplexed* (selections)
- **Secondary**:
  - *The Cambridge Companion to Medieval Philosophy*
  - Anthony Kenny: *Medieval Philosophy* (vol. 2 of *A New History of Western Philosophy*)
  - Etienne Gilson: *The Spirit of Medieval Philosophy*
  - John Marenbon: *Medieval Philosophy: An Historical and Philosophical Introduction*

## Early Modern

### Declarative
- Descartes: method of doubt, cogito, substance dualism, clear and distinct ideas, Cartesian circle, mind-body problem, mechanistic physics
- Spinoza: substance monism, God/Nature, conatus, three kinds of knowledge, Ethics as geometric method
- Leibniz: monads, pre-established harmony, principle of sufficient reason, best of all possible worlds, identity of indiscernibles
- Locke: tabula rasa, primary/secondary qualities, social contract, toleration, personal identity (memory)
- Berkeley: esse est percipi, immaterialism, bundle theory
- Hume: impressions/ideas, fork (relations of ideas vs matters of fact), problem of induction, bundle theory of self, is-ought gap, skepticism about causation

### Procedural
- Reconstruct the Cartesian method of doubt step by step
- Map Spinoza's Ethics geometrical structure (definitions → axioms → propositions)
- Analyze Hume's argument against induction and evaluate responses
- Compare rationalist and empiricist accounts of knowledge
- Write on the mind-body problem in Descartes and responses

### Theory
- **Primary texts**:
  - Descartes: *Meditations on First Philosophy* (with Objections and Replies), *Discourse on Method*, *Principles of Philosophy* (Part I)
  - Spinoza: *Ethics*, *Theological-Political Treatise* (selections)
  - Leibniz: *Monadology*, *Discourse on Metaphysics*, *New Essays on Human Understanding* (selections), *Theodicy* (selections)
  - Locke: *An Essay Concerning Human Understanding* (esp. Books I-II, IV), *Two Treatises of Government* (Second Treatise)
  - Berkeley: *Three Dialogues between Hylas and Philonous*, *A Treatise Concerning the Principles of Human Knowledge*
  - Hume: *An Enquiry Concerning Human Understanding*, *A Treatise of Human Nature* (esp. Book I), *Dialogues Concerning Natural Religion*
- **Free modernized versions**: https://www.earlymoderntexts.com/ — Jonathan Bennett's simplified editions of Descartes, Spinoza, Leibniz, Locke, Berkeley, Hume, Kant, and more
- **Secondary**:
  - *The Cambridge Companion to Descartes*, *...Spinoza*, *...Leibniz*, *...Locke*, *...Hume*
  - Anthony Kenny: *The Rise of Modern Philosophy* (vol. 3 of *A New History*)
  - Roger Woolhouse: *Descartes, Spinoza, Leibniz: The Concept of Substance*
  - Michael Ayers: *Locke* (2 vols)
  - Peter Millican: Hume lectures (Oxford podcasts)

## Kant and German Idealism

### Declarative
- Kant: synthetic a priori, transcendental idealism, phenomena/noumena, categories, transcendental deduction, transcendental unity of apperception, antinomies, categorical imperative (3 formulations), kingdom of ends, autonomy, aesthetic judgment, sublime, teleological judgment
- Fichte: the I posits itself, Wissenschaftslehre, dialectic of thesis-antithesis-synthesis (often misattributed to Hegel)
- Schelling: philosophy of nature, intellectual intuition, philosophy of identity, philosophy of art
- Hegel: Absolute Spirit, dialectic (Aufhebung), master-slave dialectic, philosophy of history, logic as metaphysics, Sittlichkeit vs Moralitat, the real is the rational

### Procedural
- Reconstruct the transcendental deduction (both A and B editions)
- Analyze and apply the categorical imperative to ethical cases
- Map the dialectical structure in Hegel's *Phenomenology* (consciousness → self-consciousness → reason → spirit)
- Write on Kant's Copernican revolution in epistemology

### Theory

  - Hegel: *Phenomenology of Spirit* (Pinkard or Miller translation), *Science of Logic* (selections), *Philosophy of Right*, *Lectures on the Philosophy of History* (Introduction)
  - Fichte: *Science of Knowledge (Wissenschaftslehre)* 1794
  - Schelling: *System of Transcendental Idealism*
- **Secondary**:
  - Henry Allison: *Kant's Transcendental Idealism* — the standard interpretation
  - Sebastian Gardner: *Kant and the Critique of Pure Reason* (Routledge Guidebook)
  - Robert Stern: *Routledge Guidebook to Hegel's Phenomenology of Spirit*
  - Frederick Beiser: *German Idealism: The Struggle against Subjectivism*, *Hegel*

## Nineteenth Century

### Declarative
- Schopenhauer: will, representation, pessimism, aesthetic contemplation, denial of the will
- Kierkegaard: three stages (aesthetic, ethical, religious), the leap of faith, anxiety, despair, subjective truth, pseudonymous authorship
- Marx: historical materialism, base/superstructure, alienation, commodity fetishism, class struggle, ideology, surplus value
- Mill: utilitarianism (higher/lower pleasures), harm principle, On Liberty, subjection of women
- Nietzsche: will to power, eternal recurrence, Ubermensch, death of God, master/slave morality, genealogy, perspectivism, amor fati, nihilism, revaluation of values
- American Pragmatism: Peirce (pragmatic maxim, fallibilism), James (will to believe, radical empiricism), Dewey (instrumentalism, democracy and education)

### Procedural

- Analyze Nietzsche's genealogical method applied to a moral concept

## Analytic Philosophy

### Declarative
- Frege: sense/reference, concept/object, logicism, compositionality, anti-psychologism
- Russell: definite descriptions, logical atomism, theory of types, Russell's paradox, knowledge by acquaintance vs description
- Early Wittgenstein: picture theory of meaning, logical space, the mystical, "Whereof one cannot speak"
- Later Wittgenstein: language games, family resemblance, rule-following, private language argument, philosophical therapy, forms of life
- Logical Positivism: verification principle, analytic/synthetic, elimination of metaphysics (Carnap, Ayer)
- Quine: "Two Dogmas of Empiricism" (no analytic/synthetic, holism), naturalized epistemology, indeterminacy of translation, ontological relativity
- Kripke: rigid designators, necessary a posteriori, possible worlds semantics, Naming and Necessity
- Later analytic: Putnam (twin earth, brain in a vat), Davidson (anomalous monism, radical interpretation), Sellars (myth of the given), Lewis (modal realism)

### Procedural
- Formalize arguments in predicate logic
- Analyze Russell's theory of definite descriptions and its problems
- Reconstruct Quine's attack on analyticity
- Write on the private language argument and its implications

### Theory

## Logic

### Declarative
- Propositional logic: connectives, truth tables, tautologies, validity, soundness
- Predicate logic: quantifiers, variables, predicates, models, validity, completeness
- Natural deduction, sequent calculus, proof theory
- Modal logic: necessity, possibility, possible worlds, Kripke semantics, S4, S5
- Philosophical logic: conditional logic, relevance logic, free logic, epistemic logic, deontic logic
- Metalogic: completeness theorem, compactness theorem, Lowenheim-Skolem
- Godel's incompleteness theorems, Church's theorem

### Procedural
- Work through logic exercises: translate English to formal logic and prove theorems
- **forall x** — exercises (free): https://www.fecundity.com/codex/forallx.pdf

- Prove basic metatheorems (soundness, completeness for propositional logic)

## Metaphysics

### Declarative
- Ontology: existence, being, substance, properties, universals vs particulars (nominalism, realism, trope theory)
- Modality: necessity, possibility, contingency, possible worlds (Lewis's modal realism, ersatzism)
- Causation: regularity theory (Hume), counterfactual (Lewis), manipulationist (Woodward), powers/dispositions
- Time: A-theory vs B-theory, presentism vs eternalism, growing block, persistence (endurantism vs perdurantism)
- Free will: compatibilism, libertarianism, hard determinism, Frankfurt cases, manipulation arguments
- Personal identity: psychological continuity (Locke/Parfit), biological (animalism), narrative, no-self (Buddhist)
- Composition: when do parts compose a whole? (universalism, nihilism, restricted composition)
- Abstract objects: Platonism, nominalism, fictionalism

### Procedural
- Construct and evaluate thought experiments (teletransportation, ship of Theseus, Chinese room, Mary's room)
- Map the logical space of positions on a metaphysical question
- Write on persistence through change using concrete examples

### Theory
- **Textbook**: *Metaphysics: A Contemporary Introduction* by Michael Loux
- **Textbook**: *Metaphysics: The Fundamentals* by Koons & Pickavance
- **Textbook**: *Riddles of Existence* by Conee & Sider — accessible
- **Textbook**: *An Introduction to Metaphysics* by Carroll & Markosian
- **Key works**: Lewis *On the Plurality of Worlds*; Kripke *Naming and Necessity*; Parfit *Reasons and Persons* (Part III); van Inwagen *Material Beings*; Sider *Four-Dimensionalism*
- **Anthology**: *Metaphysics: An Anthology* ed. Kim, Sosa, Rosenkrantz

## Epistemology

### Declarative
- JTB analysis and Gettier problems
- Foundationalism, coherentism, infinitism, reliabilism
- Internalism vs externalism about justification
- Skepticism: Cartesian, Humean, brain-in-a-vat, closure principle
- Responses to skepticism: Moorean, contextualism, relevant alternatives, safety/sensitivity conditions
- A priori/a posteriori, analytic/synthetic
- Social epistemology: testimony, disagreement, epistemic injustice (Fricker)
- Formal epistemology: Bayesianism, Dutch book arguments
- Virtue epistemology (Sosa, Zagzebski)

### Procedural
- Construct Gettier cases
- Evaluate competing theories of justification against problem cases
- Write on the regress problem and compare solutions

### Theory

- **Textbook**: *Knowledge and Its Limits* by Timothy Williamson — influential, advanced
- **Textbook**: *Epistemic Injustice* by Miranda Fricker

## Philosophy of Mind

### Declarative
- Mind-body problem: substance dualism, property dualism, identity theory, functionalism, eliminativism, anomalous monism
- Consciousness: the hard problem (Chalmers), qualia, phenomenal vs access consciousness, higher-order theories, global workspace theory, integrated information theory
- Intentionality: Brentano's thesis, content externalism (Putnam, Burge), narrow vs wide content
- Mental causation: causal exclusion problem, overdetermination, epiphenomenalism
- Key thought experiments: Mary's room (Jackson), philosophical zombies (Chalmers), Chinese room (Searle), bat (Nagel), inverted spectrum
- Free will and neuroscience: Libet experiments, compatibilist responses
- AI and consciousness: strong vs weak AI, the Chinese room, machine consciousness

### Procedural
- Evaluate the Chinese room argument step by step
- Construct a zombie argument and assess responses
- Write on whether functionalism can account for qualia

### Theory
- **Textbook**: *Philosophy of Mind: A Contemporary Introduction* by Jaegwon Kim — clear, standard
- **Textbook**: *Philosophy of Mind: Classical and Contemporary Readings* ed. Chalmers — excellent anthology
- **Textbook**: *Consciousness Explained* by Daniel Dennett
- **Key works**: Nagel "What Is It Like to Be a Bat?"; Jackson "Epiphenomenal Qualia"; Block "Troubles with Functionalism"; Chalmers *The Conscious Mind*; Searle "Minds, Brains, and Programs"; Putnam "The Nature of Mental States"; Churchland *Neurophilosophy*

## Philosophy of Language

### Declarative
- Reference: Mill (names as tags), Frege (sense/reference), Russell (descriptions), Kripke (rigid designation, causal chain), Putnam (semantic externalism)
- Meaning: truth-conditional semantics, use theory (Wittgenstein), Gricean pragmatics (implicature, maxims)
- Speech acts: Austin (locutionary/illocutionary/perlocutionary), Searle (taxonomy)
- Compositionality principle, context principle
- Indeterminacy of translation (Quine), radical interpretation (Davidson)
- Vagueness: sorites paradox, epistemicism, supervaluationism, degree theory

### Theory
- **Textbook**: *Philosophy of Language: A Contemporary Introduction* by William Lycan
- **Textbook**: *Philosophy of Language* by Scott Soames
- **Key works**: Frege "On Sense and Reference"; Russell "On Denoting"; Kripke *Naming and Necessity*; Austin *How to Do Things with Words*; Grice "Logic and Conversation"; Davidson "Truth and Meaning"; Putnam "The Meaning of 'Meaning'"
- **Anthology**: *The Philosophy of Language* ed. Martinich & Sosa

## Ethics

### Declarative
- Metaethics: moral realism (naturalism, non-naturalism), anti-realism (error theory, expressivism, quasi-realism), moral motivation (internalism/externalism), is-ought gap, open question argument
- Consequentialism: utilitarianism (act, rule, preference), objections (demandingness, integrity, justice)
- Deontology: Kantian ethics (categorical imperative, kingdom of ends, humanity formula), Rossian prima facie duties, contractualism (Scanlon)
- Virtue ethics: Aristotelian (eudaimonia, virtues, the mean, phronesis), Humean, care ethics
- Applied ethics: trolley problem, abortion (Thomson, Marquis), euthanasia, animal ethics (Singer, Regan), environmental ethics, global justice, bioethics

### Procedural
- Apply utilitarian, Kantian, and virtue ethics frameworks to the same ethical dilemma
- Reconstruct Thomson's "violinist" argument for abortion rights
- Evaluate the trolley problem and its variants
- Write a position paper defending one normative theory against objections

## Philosophy of Science

### Declarative
- Demarcation problem: what distinguishes science from non-science?
- Popper: falsifiability, conjectures and refutations, critical rationalism
- Kuhn: paradigms, normal science, anomalies, scientific revolutions, incommensurability
- Lakatos: research programmes, progressive vs degenerating
- Feyerabend: epistemological anarchism, "anything goes"
- Logical positivism: verifiability, deductive-nomological model of explanation
- Scientific realism vs anti-realism: no-miracles argument, pessimistic meta-induction, constructive empiricism (van Fraassen)
- Underdetermination, theory-ladenness of observation
- Explanation: DN model, causal-mechanical (Salmon), unificationist (Kitcher)
- Philosophy of specific sciences: philosophy of physics (spacetime, quantum mechanics), biology (natural kinds, species, adaptationism), social sciences

### Theory
- **Textbook**: *Philosophy of Science: A Very Short Introduction* by Samir Okasha — start here
- **Textbook**: *The Philosophy of Science* by Godfrey-Smith — excellent modern introduction
- **Textbook**: *Representing and Intervening* by Ian Hacking — realism debate
- **Key works**: Popper *The Logic of Scientific Discovery*; Kuhn *The Structure of Scientific Revolutions*; Lakatos "Falsification and the Methodology of Scientific Research Programmes"; Feyerabend *Against Method*; van Fraassen *The Scientific Image*; Hempel "Studies in the Logic of Confirmation"
- **Advanced**: Cartwright *How the Laws of Physics Lie*; Kitcher *The Advancement of Science*

## Philosophy of Religion

## Philosophy of Mathematics

### Declarative
- Platonism: mathematical objects exist independently (Godel, Quine-Putnam indispensability)
- Nominalism: no abstract mathematical objects (Field's fictionalism)
- Logicism: mathematics reducible to logic (Frege, Russell)
- Intuitionism: mathematics is mental construction (Brouwer, Heyting)
- Formalism: mathematics is formal manipulation of symbols (Hilbert)
- Structuralism: mathematics is about structures (Shapiro, Resnik)

### Theory
- **Textbook**: *Philosophy of Mathematics: Selected Readings* ed. Benacerraf & Putnam — the classic anthology
- **Textbook**: *Thinking about Mathematics* by Stewart Shapiro
- **Key works**: Benacerraf "What Numbers Could Not Be", "Mathematical Truth"; Godel "What Is Cantor's Continuum Problem?"; Field *Science without Numbers*; Maddy *Realism in Mathematics*
